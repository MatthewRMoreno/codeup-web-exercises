National Parks FAQ

YellowStone

  • Yellowstone national park encompasses 3,472 square miles or 2,221,766 acres, which makes it larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined
  • Yellowstone is home to more than 500 active geysers, thi is more than half of the worlds geysers.
  • Home to more than 280 species of birds.
  • More than 1,800 known archaeological sites within the park.

Yosemite

  • Granite rock formations in Yosemite glow like fire at sunset.
  • Diverse landscape supports more than 400 species.
  • Home to Yosemite Falls, one of the tallest waterfalls in the world measured at 2,425 feet.
  • One of the few places in the world where you can see a rainbow at night. If the sky is clear and the moon is full the moonlight hits the waterfalls mist and creates a rainbow.

Everglades

  • Largest remaining subtropical wilderness left in North America.
  • Contains one of the highest concentrations of species vulnerable to extinction in th United States.
  • The only place in the world where American alligators and American crocodiles coexist in the wild.
  • Supplies drinking water for more than 8 million people.
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?

What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.
